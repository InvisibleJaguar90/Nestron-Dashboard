The Reality
Most ADU buyers find out what they actually got after the project is done.
By then the contractor has moved on, the budget is spent, and what's built is what stays.
Nestron
Why It Happens
Site-built construction produces variable results, by design.
The same builder, the same plan, a different crew or season: a different outcome. Variability is structural to how site-built construction works.
Nestron
What Changes
Factory manufacturing removes that variability from the equation.
One facility, one production team, controlled conditions: the same inputs produce the same result every time. What was built is documented before the unit leaves the floor.
Nestron

What Ships to You
The unit arrives complete, not a shell.
Integrated electrical, plumbing, cabinetry, and finished interiors are already in place. It's built, inspected, and signed off before it loads onto the flatbed.

The Tradeoff
Factory-built gives up one thing: late-stage flexibility.
Decisions must be made before production begins. Once a unit enters fabrication, the design does not change. That clarity is the point, not the compromise.

Review Notes
Angle
Revised from v1. Previous version compressed the blog's process explanation into slides — a technically correct repurpose but not a platform-native one. This version enters at the audience's emotional state (anxiety about project outcomes) and uses the manufacturing argument as the answer, not the subject. The cover carries a curiosity hook that points toward the solution; the anxiety trigger is slide 2 on a neutral background with no product image to create brand confusion. The blog remains the detailed argument for readers who seek it; the carousel creates the motivation to seek it.
Arc
Curiosity hook (cover) → anxiety trigger (slide 2) → problem diagnosis (slide 3) → solution (slide 4) → proof (slide 5) → honest tradeoff (slide 6) → resolution + CTA (slide 7). The tradeoff slide (6) is intentional — naming it before the reader does builds credibility and removes the objection. Omitting it would make the post feel like marketing; including it makes it feel like useful information.
Key Metric
Saves. Slide 5 ("The unit arrives complete, not a shell") is the highest save candidate in this version — it delivers a specific, verifiable claim that buyers will want to return to or share. Slide 6 (the tradeoff) is the second highest — people save content that levels with them. Both slides should be the visual priority in production design.

Slide Design
Slides 2, 3, and 6 are pure type — off-white background (#F9F8F6), amber label (#B8722A), dark headline (#1C1C1C), Nestron wordmark bottom-right in light gray. Slides 4 and 5 follow the same design but the recommended image becomes either the background (with overlay) or a right-panel split at production. Slide 7: dark background (#1C1C1C), white type, centered. All slides: 1080 × 1350px (4:5). Minimum 5pt padding clearance around all text for safe-zone compliance.
